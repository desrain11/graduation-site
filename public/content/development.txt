# Development

Over the past two years, I have come to see design not only as a way to produce solutions, but also as a low-cost, flexible means of discovering problems, building shared visions, and exploring the unknown. I value reflective iteration because many critical insights and meaningful design experiences emerge not from constant execution, but from regularly stepping back and reinterpreting what the process reveals.

In the next few years, I hope to work in product contexts related to AI, health, or education, deepening my experience in user research, experience design, and prototyping. Over time, I want to grow from interface-centered work toward broader system-level design, contributing to product direction and cross-stakeholder alignment, and ultimately to more integrated systems that connect user needs, product goals, and social wellbeing.
