Title: Balance Stompers: A Serious Game for Children with Cerebral Palsy

Time：2024.9-2024.12

Project Type: Design Project

Role: User Experience Design \& Game Visual Design

Location: Eindhoven

Client: Adelante

EA Involved：Technology \& Realization/Creativity \& Aesthetics/User \& Society/Business \& Entrepreneurship/Math, Data \& Computing

Abstract: Balance Stompers is a rehabilitation-focused serious game built upon the Wii Balance Board, designed to enhance motivation and engagement in children with cerebral palsy during lower-limb balance training. Based on field research in rehabilitation centers, the game adapts its mechanics and interface to children’s cognitive and physical abilities, aligning therapeutic needs with playful experiences through iterative prototyping and expert feedback.

CV intro (EN):

\- Designed a serious game based on the Wii Balance Board for lower-limb rehabilitation in children with cerebral palsy aged 10–18, aiming to enhance engagement and increase motivation during physical therapy.

\- Conducted on-site research in rehabilitation centers to understand user needs and training scenarios, and developed multi-stage game mechanics and interactions based on a progressive training strategy; collaborated with therapists for multiple rounds of prototype iteration and expert evaluation.

\- Completed the design and development of two early-phase game modes: “Rise” (leg-lift control) and “Stride” (forward–backward stepping). The interface and interaction design were tailored to therapist–child collaboration in clinical environments. The game has been implemented in actual rehabilitation sessions and received positive feedback. Demo video: [https://youtu.be/W\_4xPAKyLbA](https://youtu.be/W_4xPAKyLbA)



CV intro (CN):

\- 项目基于 Wii Balance Board 设计一款用于10-18岁脑瘫儿童下肢康复训练的互动严肃游戏，提升康复参与度与训练主动性；
