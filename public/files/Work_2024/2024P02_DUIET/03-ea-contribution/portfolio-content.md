## General

Explored how a modular tangible toolkit could support beginner cooks through step-by-step guidance. Inspired by HERMITS [2], the project developed three physical modules for recipe guidance, ingredient weighing, and timing, and used 3D-printed prototypes and Wizard of Oz testing [1] to examine how interactive learning could improve clarity, engagement, and confidence in everyday cooking.

Other attempt in this course: LumiTile, an individual CV-marker experiment that introduced me to integrating simple programming into interface prototyping.

## Contribution to PI&V

- The project expanded my understanding of user interfaces beyond GUI alone.
- Wizard of Oz testing gave me a way to collect user feedback when system development was limited.

## Involved Expertise Areas

- **T&R**: modular tangible prototyping, 3D-printed prototype development, Wizard of Oz simulation
- **U&S**: user testing, observational analysis
