Title: TacoTask - A Homework Task Management Tool for Children and their Parents

Time：2025.2 - 2025.6

Project Type: Individual design research project,

Role: UX researcher

Location: Eindhoven, The Netherlands

Client: -

EA Involved：Technology & Realization/Creativity & Aesthetics/User & Society/Business & Entrepreneurship/Math, Data & Computing

Abstract: TacoTask is a hybrid homework management system that combines a mobile application with tangible components to support collaborative homework planning and execution in primary school families. The design focuses on children’s cognitive development and family interaction dynamics, aiming to explore the effectiveness of co-constructed planning and time visualization strategies in real-life homework contexts.

CV_EN：
\- Proposed and developed TacoTask, a hybrid toolkit combining a mobile application with tangible components to support collaborative homework planning and task management for primary school families.
\- Conducted co-design workshops with 6 families from China or the Netherlands to explore task breakdown and planning strategies in real-life homework scenarios, and implemented Wizard of Oz–based field testing with 3 Chinese families to explore real-world usage patterns, user experiences, and design limitations.
\- The study revealed key challenges in task breakdown and time planning during homework collaboration. It provided early validation for mechanisms such as “time visualization” and “co-constructed task planning” in alleviating homework-related stress and conflicts. Research findings were published at PervasiveHealth 2025, TacoTask – A Homework Task Management Tool for Children and their Parents
