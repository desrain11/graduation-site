## General

TacoTask is a hybrid homework management system combining a mobile app with a tangible planning toolkit, designed to help primary school families manage homework collaboratively with less stress. Following the Research through Design approach [5] with iterative development [4], the project first explored a remote monitoring concept, but expert review showed that parental surveillance can increase rather than reduce children's stress (U&S). This led to a pivot: inspired by the Pomodoro Technique [2], the redesigned system translates homework schedules into physical paper strips of varying lengths for parent-child co-planning (C&A, MD&C). Co-design workshops using Co-constructing Stories [1] with six families from China and the Netherlands (U&S) and Wizard of Oz field tests with Chinese families (U&S, T&R) [3] provided preliminary evidence of TacoTask's potential to support parent–child collaboration and reduce homework-related stress, while highlighting families' needs and expectations for homework management tools.

## Contribution to PI&V

This was my first design research project. Through practice, I gradually developed an understanding of how design can be used as a research tool, and how research can serve as a driving force for design. Through this project, I also realized that enabling users to become aware of their own stress and to proactively plan their learning at their own pace through self-regulation may help prevent greater stress from developing in the future.
