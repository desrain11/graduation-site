Title: A seat for bags: Design transformation driven by pragmatism
Time：2025.4 - 2025.6

Project Type: Elective course project

Role: Individual Project

Location: Eindhoven, The Netherlands

Client: -

EA Involved: Creativity & Aesthetics

Abstract: Ground or not the ground? What is your truth for the seat of your bags? 
When entering a new environment, where would you choose to place your backpack and other belongings? On the floor, or on non-ground places like stools, tables, or sofas? This project begins with the topic "A Seat for Bags," deeply reflects on the philosophy of Pragmatism by James [1], and goes through four rounds of design iterations with a studio group. This journey started from a designer's personal empathy towards bag placement behaviors in new a new country, an new environment, and eventually explores a Pragmatist interpretation of "the truth for the seat of guests' bags." This design has experienced and inspired reflection on the importance of growing truths in future design practices.
[1]	James, W. 2014. Pragmatism: A New Name for Some Old Ways of Thinking.
