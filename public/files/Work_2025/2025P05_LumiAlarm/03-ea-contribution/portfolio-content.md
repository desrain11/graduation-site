## General

Applied a Data-enabled Design approach [1] to explore sleep behaviour in context through probe deployment. Starting from environmental sensing and later shifting toward awake-in-bed behaviour, the project developed Lumi Alarm, a light-based inform probe that used ambient cues to reduce non-sleep activities in bed and support healthier bedtime routines.

## Contribution to PI&V

- The project helped me treat data as a design material rather than only a measurement outcome.
- Probes and diaries made everyday sleep behaviour visible in a more situated and reflective way.

## Involved Expertise Areas

- **MD&C**: Data-enabled Design principles, probe-based data collection, diary-supported data interpretation
- **T&R**: context exploration probe, informed exploration probe, ambient-light interaction prototyping
- **U&S**: co-constructing stories (field testing)
