## General

Designed a data-driven digital twin system for improving sleep comfort in a smart-home context by connecting bedroom conditions, sleep-monitoring data, daily routines, and user preferences. The project explored how a digital representation of the bedroom could identify patterns affecting sleep and generate adaptive recommendations for a more personalized sleep environment.

## Contribution to PI&V

- The digital twin is not only something to design, but also a data-driven way of thinking about system structure.
- The course strengthened my interest in using data-driven systems thinking to support everyday well-being.

## Involved Expertise Areas

- **MD&C**: sleep-data interpretation, digital twin role definition, data visualization, pattern-based personalization
- **T&R**: smart-home digital twin system design, data-informed interface prototyping
