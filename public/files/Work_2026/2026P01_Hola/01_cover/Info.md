Title: Hola the AI Healing Chatbot: Designing Controllable AI Memory for Self-Disclosure Contexts

Time：2026.2-2026.6

Project Type: Graduation Project (Design & Design Research)

Role: UX Researcher

Location: Eindhoven

Client: Nexo Intelligent Technology Limited

EA Involved：Technology & Realization/Creativity & Aesthetics/User & Society/Business & Entrepreneurship/Math, Data & Computing

Abstract: Hola is an AI healing chatbot designed to support young adults in everyday self-disclosure conversations. This Final Master Project focuses on the personalization of Hola’s responses, especially how AI memory can shape users’ communication experience. In the previous M21 phase, design iterations explored the dimensions and control methods of response personalization. Preliminary testing suggested that participants generally valued an AI agent with a stronger sense of memory. However, this also raised a new design question: in self-disclosure contexts, how much should an AI remember, and how should users control the way past information is brought back into conversation?
To explore this, the project designed a memory strength control mechanism inspired by the Ebbinghaus forgetting curve. Instead of using a simple on/off memory switch or asking users to edit individual memories, the prototype allows users to adjust how strongly Hola recalls past conversations. A field test then examined how this control affected perceived communication quality, willingness to self-disclose, and users’ expectations of AI memory.
